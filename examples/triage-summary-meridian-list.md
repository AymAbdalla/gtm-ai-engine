## 🟡 Qualify (4) — with discovery questions

**Northgate Logistics** — 3,800 EE, logistics tech. The org is frontline-heavy, but there's a ~1,200-person desk HQ. The account lives or dies on the segment play:
1. What's the desk vs. frontline split, and are they on the same systems today?
2. Does the HQ/corporate org own its own people-systems decisions?
3. Who felt the pain last time a people-process broke at HQ?
4. Any appetite to modernize corporate first and leave frontline tooling in place?

**Vantage Grid** — 5,600 EE, energy software. Above the sweet spot; only a division play works:
1. Any business units or acquired entities running independently of the corporate stack?
2. Which teams does the current system underserve most?
3. Has any group already gone around IT for its own people tooling? (Shadow tools = the entry point.)
4. What would a pilot inside one division need to prove?

**Bluepine Hospitality Group** — 2,900 EE, hospitality. Band fits; workforce mix could DQ it:
1. Corporate vs. property-staff split?
2. Where does HQ people-admin live today?
3. Two boutique-brand acquisitions in 2025 — integrated, or still running their own people processes?
4. If frontline stays on current tooling, is a corporate-only platform decision realistic?

**Summit Legal Group** — 950 EE, legal services. Below the enterprise band but on spreadsheets and hiring:
1. What's the growth plan for the next 18 months?
2. Who would sponsor an HR-systems move — managing partner, COO, or an HR lead?
3. What breaks first as you cross 1,000 people?
